Why we have who we do?

The U.S. Army Junior Reserve Officer Training Corps (JROTC) is a program offered to high schools that teaches students character education, student achievement, wellness, leadership, and diversity. It is a cooperative effort between the U.S. Army and the high schools to produce successful students and citizens, while fostering in each school a more constructive and disciplined learning environment. The outcomes of the JROTC program are: Act with integrity and personal accountability as they lead others to succeed in a diverse and global workforce Engage civic and social concerns in the community, government, and society Graduate prepared to excel in post-secondary options and career pathways Make decisions that promote positive social, emotional, and physical health Value the role of the military and other service organizations With the schoolâ€™s support, the JROTC program achieves these outcomes by using a world-class 21st Century, technology driven, student centered curriculum. The curriculum consists of education in citizenship, leadership, social and communication skills, physical fitness and wellness, geography, and civics.

Who makes us?

We have almost 130 active cadets in the rotc program. Each cadet holds a rank and current position within the program. With these positions come duties and specific task that each cadet should be able to perform and/or actively performs.
